# OpenTopology IPR and Patent Policy

OpenTopology is intended to be implementable on a royalty-free basis.

This policy applies to contributions that are accepted into this repository,
including specification text, schemas, examples, reference code, tests, and
tooling.

This document is a project policy, not legal advice.

## Goals

The goals of this policy are to:

- allow independent implementations of OpenTopology
- reduce patent uncertainty for implementers
- keep normative specifications usable by open source and proprietary projects
- make contributor expectations explicit before adoption grows

## Copyright Licensing

Accepted specification prose is licensed under CC-BY-4.0.

Accepted schemas, examples, reference code, tests, and tooling are licensed
under Apache-2.0.

## Royalty-Free Patent Commitment

By intentionally submitting a contribution for inclusion in OpenTopology, you
agree that, to the extent you control the relevant patent rights, you grant a
worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge, royalty-free patent license to implement,
use, make, have made, sell, offer to sell, import, and distribute
implementations of the accepted contribution as incorporated into an
OpenTopology specification or required schema.

This commitment applies only to patent claims that are necessarily infringed by
implementing the accepted contribution as incorporated into the applicable
OpenTopology specification. These are referred to here as essential claims.

## Scope

The royalty-free commitment covers:

- accepted normative specification contributions
- accepted schema semantics required by the specification
- accepted examples or reference assets only where they define required
  implementation behavior

The commitment does not cover:

- optional implementation techniques not required by the specification
- independent products, services, or technologies merely referenced by the
  specification
- claims that would be infringed only by combining OpenTopology with unrelated
  technology
- trademark rights

## Defensive Suspension

If a party asserts a patent claim against an OpenTopology implementation based
on implementing the OpenTopology specification, any patent licenses granted to
that party under this policy may be suspended or terminated to the extent
permitted by law and applicable license terms.

## Normative Contributions

Maintainers may require additional confirmation before accepting normative
changes, especially from contributors acting on behalf of an employer or other
legal entity.

That confirmation may include:

- a signed contributor agreement
- an employer authorization statement
- an explicit IPR disclosure
- a statement that the contributor is not aware of essential claims that would
  block royalty-free implementation

## Disclosures

Contributors should disclose any patent, licensing, or third-party rights issue
they believe may affect implementation of a proposed contribution.

Disclosures should be made in the relevant issue or pull request unless a
private disclosure is necessary. Private disclosures may be sent to the project
maintainers listed in `GOVERNANCE.md`.

## Relationship to Apache-2.0

Apache-2.0 already includes a patent grant for covered implementation assets.
This policy adds a standards-oriented royalty-free commitment for essential
claims in accepted specification contributions.
