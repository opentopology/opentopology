# OpenTopology Trademark Policy

This policy protects the meaning of the OpenTopology name while allowing fair
and accurate references to the project.

The names "OpenTopology", "otop", and OpenTopology compatibility marks are
project marks. This policy also applies to logos or visual marks if adopted.

## Allowed Uses

You may use the marks to:

- refer to the OpenTopology project
- describe compatibility with a specific OpenTopology version
- identify unmodified copies of OpenTopology materials
- discuss, review, teach, or compare OpenTopology

Examples:

- "This tool exports OpenTopology Core 0.1 graphs."
- "This library validates otop-core 0.1 manifests."
- "Compatible with OpenTopology Core 0.1."

## Compatibility Claims

Compatibility claims must be accurate, specific, and testable. They should name
the version and profile implemented.

Do not claim generic "OpenTopology compatible" status if the implementation
only supports a subset, extension, draft, or modified version.

## Not Allowed Without Permission

You may not use the marks to:

- imply project endorsement
- imply maintainer certification
- name an unrelated product, company, or service
- present a modified specification as the official OpenTopology specification
- make misleading compatibility or conformance claims

## Forks and Modified Versions

Forks and modified versions may explain that they are based on OpenTopology,
but they must not present themselves as the official project or specification.

All rights not granted by this policy are reserved.
